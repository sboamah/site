ReCollect

Role: Mobile UI Developer
Type of Project: Academic (Senior Design Project)
Technologies: Figma, Flutter, Firebase, Github

Objective: To create a mobile application that enables users with memory-related ailments to connect with their families.

Timeline: September 2021 to May 2022

Background

From May 2019 - Jan 2020, I worked in a senior care facility where I provided activities for individuals of various ages and abilities. I discovered my passion for accessibility through working with Dementia patients. Over 6 million Americans are living with Alzheimer’s Disease (Alzheimer’s Association). The time spent away from their families further deteriorated the connections between individuals with Dementia and their loved ones as these individuals' memories faded over the years.

Through my Senior Design project, I researched strategies for accessibility, implemented user-centered design for vulnerable populations, and gained insights into how software can particularly benefit neurodivergent individuals and individuals with memory-related issues.

Research

Our team focused on the second pillar of Alzheimer’s prevention: Social Engagement. We first looked at publications on reminiscence therapy. Reminiscence therapy refers to treatment that uses all five senses to help individuals with Dementia better maintain memories. Due to being limited to electronic devices, we focused on the effects of visual and audio tools to treat patients diagnosed with Alzheimer’s Disease.

During our initial research, we had the support of two medical professionals and two assisted livings. I interviewed employees of local assisted living facilities to understand the relationships between facility residents and their families, and the influence of the COVID-19 pandemic and technology on these relationships.

I also conducted my own secondary research about color theory to better inform our design system later on in development. Blue was the most popular color among all ages, so I factored that in when designing and implementing an application for families to enjoy. Lastly, it was important to approach designing this application with accessibility in mind, while recognizing the harm in confusing age with accessibility. I ensured that the application catered to the needs of our older population while maintaining its appeal to all users.

Development

The first four (4) weeks of the project lifecycle was dedicated to planning. The team consisted of five (5) software engineers with front-end, full-stack, and back-end experience. We met over Zoom and established our meeting structure of every Tuesday and Thursday, which included sprint reviews and refining our backlog. We spent two weeks to finalize our project proposal.
The finalized project proposal was a mobile application that allows caregivers of someone with Alzheimer's, Dementia, or CTE to curate photos, audios, and videos to rejog their love one's memories. The goal is to help patients revive lost and forgotten memories while also being able to track how well the person is remembering their memories through data collection.

The following weeks until May consisted of implementation, testing, crafting our business pitch, and showing five demos. My main focus was designing mockups in Figma and UI development in Flutter. Our core functionality was completed and ready to show in Stevens' Innovation Expo 2022.

Takeaways

  • Design Systems: Constructed custom reusable widgets and styles compatible with iOS and Android devices, cutting UI development by 50% each month.
  • Collaboration: Worked with a team of five peers along with several different stakeholders.
  • Empathy: Learned about the experiences of individuals with memory-related ailments and how to advocate for their needs within the digital space.
